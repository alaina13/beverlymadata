Overview

The City of Beverly last published citywide pavement condition data in Fall 2021, when its Engineering Department surveyed all city streets using a Pavement Condition Index (PCI) scored 0–100. That survey is now over four years old. The Massachusetts Department of Transportation (MassDOT) separately assesses pavement condition on federal-aid-eligible roads statewide using a Pavement Serviceability Index (PSI), most recently surveying Beverly in Fall 2024. MassDOT's data is more current but narrower in scope: it covers 26.8 miles of Beverly's roads, about 17% of the city's estimated 160-mile total road network, concentrated on arterials and major collectors, and does not include most local residential streets that were part of the city's 2021 survey. The two indices use different scales and methodologies and are not directly comparable.

Methodology & Scope

What this data does and doesn't cover

MassDOT's Pavement Serviceability Index (PSI) runs from 0 (impassable) to 5 (free of distress) and combines cracking, rutting, and ride quality, measured as International Roughness Index (IRI). The Interstate system is surveyed annually; other federal-aid roads follow a biennial schedule.

This dataset only covers roads classified as arterial or collector under the Federal Functional Classification system. It excludes locally classified roads, which make up most of Beverly's residential street network and are not part of MassDOT's assessment program. Yankee Division Highway (Route 128) is included as its own row in the table above: it is a state-owned, limited-access highway rather than a surface street, and its condition is not representative of Beverly's other roads.

Condition tiers (Poor / Fair / Good / Excellent) are MassDOT's own classification, assigned per road segment based on PSI. Averages on this page are weighted by segment length.
