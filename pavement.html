Overview

Beverly has pavement condition data for 1,694 street segments across all six wards, about 130.7 miles of road. The city's Engineering and GIS departments keep it current through 2024. Until now, it hasn't been easy to find. The data lives inside a GIS tool built for city staff, not a public webpage. Open Beverly pulls it out so residents can search their own street below.

Covers 581 streets and 1,694 tracked segments citywide, from the city's own pavement condition inventory. Streets with multiple blocks may vary in condition block to block; see Methodology & Scope.

Methodology & Scope

What this data does and doesn't cover

The city's Pavement Condition Index (PCI) runs from 0 (failed) to 100 (excellent) and is grouped into six tiers: Excellent (86–100), Good (71–85), Fair (56–70), Poor (41–55), Very Poor (26–40), and Serious (17–25).

The city's inventory includes a "pci_2025" field with decimal values (for example, 87.89) that appear to be a deterioration-model projection rather than a fresh physical survey: every segment in the dataset shares the same underlying survey date. This page uses each segment's 2024 PCI value, the most recent figure tied to an actual assessment, as the current condition.

The city's GIS system was last updated on July 9, 2026, though the underlying assessments are current through the 2024 survey cycle. Some fields in the data schema, including repair priority, estimated repair cost, and maintenance notes, exist but were not populated for any segment as of this page's last data pull.

Address search matches each property to its nearest surveyed pavement segment, using the city's parcel location data. This is a distance-based estimate, not an exact address-to-segment mapping from the city: for most addresses the match is within a few dozen feet of the property, but on long streets with sparse survey coverage, or for addresses on private ways and unaccepted streets, no confident match exists. About 93% of parcel addresses match within 150 meters of a surveyed segment; the rest fall back to full street-level results.

Data sources
