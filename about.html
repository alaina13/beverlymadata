Who made this site, why, and how.

About this site

I'm Alaina Pitt, a Beverly homeowner and parent. I kept hearing claims about Beverly's budget, the City Hall renovation, trash fees, and the structural deficit, and wanted to see the data behind them. So I built this, for myself and for anyone else who wants to dig in. It aims to be factual and nonpartisan. It also reflects a specific lens: long-term fiscal sustainability, and what the numbers say about how a city grows.

Open Beverly is an independent civic project. I'm not on any city boards or commissions, and I'm not affiliated with any advocacy group related to Beverly.

How I use AI

This site was built with help from Claude, an AI assistant made by Anthropic. I use it to draft content, process data, and build the site itself. Claude has read through nine years of Financial Forecasting Committee minutes, annual financial reports, budget documents, and parcel data for the entire city.

I review and edit AI-generated content before it goes up, with one exception: meeting summaries. Those are generated automatically from BevCam recordings and published without manual review. They can contain errors: mishearing names, misattributing statements, missing context. Treat them as a starting point, not a record.

If something looks wrong anywhere on the site, I want to know.
