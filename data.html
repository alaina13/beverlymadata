Free cash and the stabilization fund are not the same thing

Free cash is the unspent balance from the prior fiscal year, certified annually by the Massachusetts Department of Revenue. It is generated when actual revenues exceed estimates or when budgeted spending goes unspent. Beverly has certified approximately $10–11M in free cash each year from FY2023 through FY2026. Free cash can be appropriated for one-time uses, such as capital projects, reserve transfers, or gap-filling, but cannot be treated as a permanent revenue source.

The stabilization fund is a formal reserve account that the city builds up over time by transferring free cash into it. Beverly grew its stabilization fund from near zero in FY2009 to $18.6M by FY2023. City policy targets keeping the fund at 3–8% of the general fund. Unlike free cash, the stabilization fund requires a two-thirds vote of the City Council to appropriate.

Beverly has used both to help balance budgets during years of structural pressure. The Financial Forecasting Committee noted in December 2025 that this strategy has limits: drawing reserves down to cover recurring gaps eventually exhausts the buffer without fixing the underlying math.

New Growth & the Tax Levy

Certified figures from the Massachusetts Department of Revenue Tax Rate Recapitulation and Levy Limit worksheets for Beverly (municipal code 030), FY2020–FY2026.

"New growth" is the amount a city is allowed to add to its tax levy limit each year from new construction and other real additions to the tax base, separate from the standard 2.5% increase Proposition 2½ allows on the existing base. Beverly's certified new growth averaged $1.63 million per year from FY2020 through FY2026, ranging from $1.37 million (FY2022) to $2.10 million (FY2024). New growth is added to the levy limit as a flat dollar amount; it does not compound into the 2.5% base increase. See Key Concepts for full definitions of levy limit and new growth.

Revenue by Source

Massachusetts Department of Revenue, Division of Local Services, Municipal Databank. Beverly (municipal code 030), FY2016–FY2026.

Beverly's total municipal revenue grew from $134.8 million in FY2016 to $200.9 million in FY2026, an increase of 49.1%. The tax levy is consistently the largest source, ranging from 65.1% to 69.3% of total revenue over the period. State aid's share rose from 11.1% in FY2016 to 12.3% in FY2026. Enterprise and Community Preservation Act (CPA) funds declined from 12.4% to 10.7% of the total over the same period.
