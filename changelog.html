Changelog

What's changed on Open Beverly.

July 16, 2026
Property map layer panel

Rebuilt the property map sidebar as a layer panel with collapsible rows instead of stacked, always-visible sections.

  • Added a zoning overlay (zoning districts, overlay/historic districts) sourced from BeverlyGIS.
  • Parcel value and zoning districts are mutually exclusive fills so their color palettes never compete; overlay/historic districts render as outlines that can stack on top of either.

Fixes:

  • Disabled the parcel value controls (color-by, underperforming toggle, filter-by-type) when the Parcel value layer is hidden, since they previously had no visible effect while that layer was off.
July 15, 2026
Daily tracking for the Street Paving page

Added daily tracking of the City of Beverly's Street Paving page, recording a new history entry only when the page's content changes.

July 13, 2026
Pavement Conditions rebuilt on city data, Meetings and Agendas merged

Rebuilt the Pavement Conditions page around the City of Beverly Engineering/GIS Department's own pavement condition inventory (1,694 segments across all 6 wards), replacing the MassDOT arterial-only survey used at launch.

  • Search by street name or address, matching each address to its nearest surveyed segment and showing the street average alongside it.
  • Added a donut chart of road miles by condition tier.
  • Added a bar chart of average condition by road classification, plus below-standard mileage compared against the Massachusetts Municipal Association's statewide annual upkeep benchmark.
  • Renamed the page and moved it into a new Infrastructure nav section alongside the Capital Plan.
  • Featured as an interactive tool on the homepage.

Merged the Meetings and Agendas pages: upcoming meetings now link directly to their agenda or minutes, matched by AgendaCenter document ID. Added one-click Google Calendar and downloadable .ics links to each event. The agendas archive moved to a section on the same page.

Fixes:

  • Fixed multi-word street search on the Pavement Conditions page.
  • Fixed whitespace-only street names appearing in pavement search results.
  • Narrowed Upcoming Meetings cards to match content width.
  • Forced the homepage featured tools grid to a fixed 2x2 layout.
July 12, 2026
Added Pavement Conditions page

Added the Pavement Conditions page using MassDOT's Pavement Serviceability Index survey for Beverly's arterial and collector roads (26.8 of approximately 160 total city road miles), since the city had not published its own road-by-road condition data at the time. Cross-linked from the Capital Plan page and added to the Finance nav.

Corrected the page same day to cite the city's own Fall 2021 PCI survey, which does exist but is outdated, rather than implying no city data had ever existed. Rebuilt the next day around the city's own live pavement data; see July 13, 2026.

July 10, 2026
Site favicon

Added a favicon (OB monogram, navy/orange brand colors) with icon and apple-touch-icon links on every page.

July 9, 2026
Revenue by Source on City Data page

Added a Revenue by Source section to the City Data page: a stacked bar chart and percentage breakdown table of Beverly's municipal revenue by category (Tax Levy, State Aid, Local Receipts, Enterprise & CPA Funds, All Other) for FY2016-FY2026, sourced from the MA DLS Municipal Databank.

July 8, 2026
Readability rewrite, deficit trajectory chart, and accessibility fixes

Rewrote explanatory text for easier reading. No figures, quotes, or sourcing changed.

  • History page ("How We Got Here"): simplified the structural deficit explainer, the year-by-year narrative, and chart captions.
  • Key Concepts page: simplified glossary definitions for structural deficit, General Fund, Enterprise Fund, free cash, stabilization fund, ACFR, FFC, levy limit, new growth, debt exclusion, and excess levy capacity.
  • About page: tightened copy to a more direct, first-person voice, lowering the reading level from grade 12.2 to 9.9 (Flesch-Kincaid). No facts changed.

New features:

  • Added a revenue-vs-expenditure trajectory chart to the Deficit Trajectory section on the History page.
  • Converted the History page's yearly timeline entries into collapsible details, replacing the wall-of-text layout. Applied the same pattern to the Override Calculator's assumptions list.

Accessibility fixes:

  • Added the screen-reader chart data table pattern (used on every other chart page) to the Override Calculator's two charts.
  • Added an aria-live region to the property map's parcel detail panel so screen readers announce updates when a parcel is selected.
  • Added site-wide support for prefers-reduced-motion, disabling non-functional animations and transitions for users who request reduced motion at the OS level.
July 7, 2026
Neutrality revisions, new Capital Plan page, and map fixes

Revised the following to be more neutral:

  • Home page
  • Capital Plan page
  • History page
  • What's Next page
    • City Hall renovation section

New features:

  • Added a Feedback link to the footer on every page.
  • Added a "Request a feature" option on the About page.
  • Added the Capital Plan page.

Fixes:

  • Added a close button to the mobile map sidebar.
  • Fixed map tooltips getting cut off near the edge of the screen.
  • Fixed a bug where closing a parcel panel on the map could break subsequent searches.
  • Fixed footnote numbering on the History and What's Next pages.
  • Improved error handling when property data fails to load on the Override Calculator.
